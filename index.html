May 2022

Prescriptive Analysis
of lost species

An analysis of all terrestrial vertebrates listed in the IUCN redlist. We identified a total of 562 'lost' species (those which have not been observered in over 50 years). Of these, only 13% (75 species) are listed as ‘Possibly Extinct’ by the IUCN. We recorded species locations and looked at their correlation with mega-diverse countries, along with patterns associated with taxonomy and enviroment. We suggest minor adjustments to IUCN Red List accounts to allow lost species to be better tracked, including more consistent use of the ‘Possibly Extinct’ marker and wider application of the ‘last seen date’ field.

March 2023

Predictive Modelling
of NBA game outcomes

This Project is based on NBA game data for the 2022-2023 season. The main goal was to build a model to predict the winner of any matchup based on past team performance from this season. Using past game data accessed through the NBA API, I engineered a rolling average plus-minus score differential variable to compare the teams recent performances coming into new games. I added a back-to-back game variable to further refine the model and improve its accuracy. I used a gradient boosting algorithm trained on past game data for the 2022-2023 season and achieved a correct prediction percentage of 60.3% when used on a test data set.
